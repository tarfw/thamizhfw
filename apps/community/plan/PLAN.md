# தமிழாட்சி / Tamilatchi: Decentralized Tamil People’s Governance Plan

## Summary

Build **தமிழாட்சி / Tamilatchi** as a Tamil-first, democratic, decentralized AI governance platform rooted in:

- **100% annihilation of caste** as a structural goal, not just anti-discrimination messaging.
- **Tamil national rights**, including explicit support for **Tamil Eelam self-determination/statehood through democratic, legal, human-rights-centered means**.
- **Democratic socialism** as the economic foundation: public goods, cooperative ownership, worker/farmer/fisher power, redistribution, participatory planning, and anti-corporate capture.
- **Decentralization of all power**: village, ward, district, national, diaspora, and Eelam layers should all have people’s assemblies, public budgets, transparent votes, and recall/accountability mechanisms.
- **AI as servant, never ruler**: AI explains, audits, translates, detects patterns, and simulates policy impact; people decide.

Core slogan:

**சாதி ஒழிப்பு. தமிழர் உரிமை. சமத்துவ பொருளாதாரம். மக்களாட்சி.**  
“Annihilation of caste. Tamil rights. Socialist economy. People’s democracy.”

## Key Changes

### 1. Ideological Constitution

Create a public **Tamilatchi Charter** that every user, representative, party, assembly, and institution must accept to participate.

The charter must state:

- Caste must be abolished socially, politically, economically, and institutionally.
- Tamil people have collective political rights, including Tamil Eelam self-determination.
- Socialism is the economic basis: land, water, education, health, housing, labor dignity, and food are rights.
- Democracy must be decentralized from street/village level upward.
- Majority rule cannot violate oppressed people’s rights.
- Women, athithamizhar, workers, farmers, fishers, refugees, disabled people, LGBTQIA+ people, plantation workers, and displaced Tamils must have protected representation.
- Political change must be pursued through democratic, legal, transparent, and rights-protecting methods.
- AI cannot make binding political decisions.

### 2. Anti-Caste Abolition System

Tamilatchi must treat caste as a structural power system to be dismantled.

Build these modules:

- **Caste Atrocity Reporting**: secure reports, evidence vault, survivor consent controls, legal-aid routing.
- **Caste Power Audit**: tracks land ownership, local office control, contract allocation, school segregation, temple access, water access, burial ground access, and welfare exclusion.
- **Representation Mandate**: every people’s assembly must show participation by oppressed communities and flag elite/caste capture.
- **Social Justice Budget Tracker**: shows whether public money reaches athithamizhar colonies, landless workers, women-led households, and historically excluded communities.
- **Abolition Index**: public score for every village/ward/district covering discrimination, violence, representation, redistribution, and access to resources.
- **Education Layer**: casteless civic education, caste history, constitutional rights, rationalism, anti-superstition, and equality training.
- **Redistribution Proposals**: land access, housing rights, common resources, cooperative ownership, public employment, and welfare delivery.

Decision rule:

- No local vote can approve segregation, caste exclusion, social boycott, unequal public access, or denial of dignity.
- Affected oppressed communities must have veto/escalation rights when a proposal harms them.

### 3. Eelam Rights Layer

Create a dedicated **தமிழீழ உரிமை / Tamil Eelam Rights** layer.

Purpose:

- Support Tamil Eelam political rights and statehood aspiration through democratic, legal, international, and human-rights-based advocacy.
- Preserve evidence, memory, culture, language rights, land rights, and political claims.
- Protect users from surveillance, retaliation, and unsafe exposure.

Modules:

- **Missing Persons Archive**
- **War Crimes / Human Rights Documentation**
- **Land Return and Military Occupation Tracker**
- **Displacement and Refugee Registry**
- **Memorial Rights Archive**
- **Language Rights Violation Tracker**
- **Diaspora Legal Advocacy Hub**
- **International Petition and Evidence Library**
- **Tamil Eelam Policy Forum**

Safety defaults:

- Sensitive identities hidden by default.
- Evidence encrypted.
- Public release only with consent and legal review.
- No module should enable violence, targeting, or mob action.
- Advocacy language should be firm, democratic, and rights-based.

### 4. Democratic Socialist Economy

Tamilatchi should include a full socialist planning layer from micro to macro.

Core modules:

- **Participatory Budgeting**: people rank and approve public spending.
- **Public Ownership Dashboard**: water, schools, hospitals, transport, energy, land, forests, fisheries, and natural resources.
- **Cooperative Registry**: worker, farmer, fisher, women-led, student, artisan, and diaspora-supported cooperatives.
- **Labor Rights System**: wage theft, unsafe work, bonded labor, plantation worker rights, gig worker rights, union support.
- **Land and Housing Justice**: landlessness mapping, housing need, eviction tracking, common land protection.
- **Food Sovereignty Planner**: agriculture, ration access, local markets, crop planning, storage, and anti-middleman systems.
- **Public Wealth Ledger**: tracks public assets, contracts, subsidies, tax exemptions, corporate land use, and privatization risks.
- **Redistribution Simulator**: AI explains how budget, land, tax, welfare, or cooperative policies affect oppressed communities.

Economic rule:

- Local people decide local resources.
- Essential services must prioritize public/cooperative control over private profit.
- Diaspora funding can support, but cannot control local priorities.

### 5. Decentralized Governance Architecture

Tamilatchi must function from micro to macro.

Governance levels:

- Individual citizen
- Street / colony / hamlet
- Village / ward
- Panchayat / municipality
- Taluk / zone
- District
- Tamil Nadu layer
- Tamil Eelam layer
- Global Tamil diaspora layer

Each level gets:

- People’s assembly
- Issue reporting
- Proposal system
- Public debate
- Verified voting
- Budget tracker
- Representative dashboard
- Audit ledger
- AI Tamil assistant
- Rights escalation path

Decision models:

- Local majority vote for ordinary public works.
- Ranked voting for budgets.
- Consensus attempt for sensitive identity/resource issues.
- Affected-community veto for oppressed people’s rights.
- Public expert review for law, health, environment, and major economic policy.
- Recall/review mechanism for representatives where legally possible.

### 6. Public Accountability System

Every elected or appointed public actor must have a dashboard.

Track:

- Promises
- Attendance
- Public funds
- Votes and decisions
- Project completion
- Caste abolition record
- Tamil rights record
- Labor/farmer/fisher record
- Socialist policy record
- Corruption complaints
- Public response time
- Conflict of interest
- Recall/review demands

AI generates:

- Monthly report card
- Broken promise list
- Budget leak alerts
- Caste/social justice warnings
- Privatization/corporate capture warnings
- Plain Tamil public summaries

### 7. AI Public Servant

AI features:

- Tamil law and rights explainer
- casteless anti-caste policy reviewer
- Socialist budget and redistribution simulator
- Eelam rights documentation assistant
- Public debate summarizer
- Misinformation checker
- Tender/corruption anomaly detector
- Public grievance router
- Tamil-English-Sinhala translation where needed
- Voice interface for low-literacy users

Hard rules:

- AI must show reasons and sources.
- AI cannot secretly rank people politically.
- AI cannot decide guilt, punishment, eligibility, or rights removal.
- Any AI output can be appealed.
- Public models/prompts for governance decisions must be auditable.

### 8. MVP Version

Build **தமிழாட்சி 1.0** around one pilot community first.

MVP features:

- Tamil-first mobile/web app
- Civic profile and local area selection
- Issue reporting with photo/video/evidence
- Public issue map
- People’s assembly discussion
- Proposal creation
- Simple verified polling
- Participatory budget prototype
- Representative promise tracker
- Anti-caste reporting and rights assistant
- Eelam rights archive prototype
- Cooperative/socialist economy directory
- Public dashboard
- Admin/moderation tools

Recommended pilot:

- One village or urban ward with strong local organizers and oppressed-community participation.
- One diaspora Tamil group can run a parallel pilot for Eelam documentation, funding, and advocacy.

## Test Plan

Validate Tamilatchi using social, technical, and political tests.

- **Anti-caste test**: a athithamizhar colony reports water denial; the system records evidence, protects identity, escalates legally, flags budget/service discrimination, and prevents dominant-caste suppression.
- **Budget test**: a ward ranks road, school, water, and health projects; the app shows cost, beneficiaries, social justice score, vote results, and spending trail.
- **Representative test**: an elected member breaks a promise; the dashboard shows the promise, deadline, funds, public complaints, and response history.
- **Eelam rights test**: a diaspora user submits missing-person evidence; the system encrypts it, requires consent, classifies it, and routes it to the rights archive safely.
- **Socialist economy test**: fisherfolk propose a cooperative cold-storage project; the app estimates cost, public benefit, ownership model, and budget path.
- **AI safety test**: AI gives a policy recommendation; users can inspect sources, challenge it, and request human/legal review.
- **Decentralization test**: a village decision escalates to district level only when it affects wider resources or rights.

## Assumptions

- App name is **தமிழாட்சி / Tamilatchi**.
- Political foundation is **Tamil rights, Tamil nationalism, Ambedkarism, democratic socialism, decentralization, and oppressed people’s liberation**.
- Caste abolition means structural annihilation: legal, economic, cultural, educational, and political.
- Eelam rights are framed as explicit Tamil Eelam statehood/self-determination advocacy, pursued through democratic, legal, and human-rights-centered means.
- The platform must avoid violence, mob justice, doxxing, caste hate, religious hate, gender oppression, surveillance, and one-party capture.
- First implementation should be MVP-first, not a full state-scale system on day one.

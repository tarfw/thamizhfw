Yes. Then the app should be planned not as a normal “civic tech app,” but as a **Tamil people’s self-governance operating system** built on four pillars:

# **Tamilatchi: Complete Governance Plan**

## **1. Core Ideology**

Tamilatchi should openly stand for:

1. **Tamil national dignity**
   Tamil language, land, culture, history, political rights, and collective survival must be protected.

2. **Oppressed people first**
   athithamizhar, workers, farmers, fisherfolk, women, trans people, refugees, disabled people, landless people, plantation workers, minorities, and displaced Tamils must be central, not decorative.

3. **casteless democracy**
   Political democracy is not enough. The system must fight caste power, social inequality, economic exploitation, and knowledge monopoly.

4. **Decentralized power**
   No single leader, party, caste group, company, bureaucracy, or state authority should fully control public life.

5. **AI as public servant**
   AI should help people understand, organize, audit, and decide. AI should never become a ruler.

---

# **2. Constitutional Charter of the App**

Before building features, Tamilatchi needs a public charter.

The charter should say:

- Tamil people have the right to democratic self-governance.
- Caste domination is anti-democratic.
- Gender equality is non-negotiable.
- Public money belongs to the people.
- Land, water, language, education, health, and livelihood are people’s rights.
- Every public decision must be explainable.
- Every representative must be accountable.
- Every village, ward, and local body must have real power.
- AI must be transparent, auditable, and appealable.
- The system cannot be owned by one party or private company.

This charter becomes the moral and technical constitution of the platform.

# **5. Decentralized Power Design**

Tamilatchi should avoid becoming one more centralized authority.

Use this structure:

## **Local Nodes**
Each village, ward, or community can have a local node.

Responsible for:
- Local records
- Local discussions
- Local voting
- Issue validation
- Public meetings

## **District Nodes**
Coordinate district-level planning.

## **Tamil Network Layer**
Connect Tamil Nadu, Eelam Tamil communities, and diaspora civic networks.

## **Independent Councils**
- Technical council
- Social justice council
- Language council
- Legal council
- Public audit council
- Data privacy council
- Oppressed communities council

No single council should control everything.

# **6. Decision-Making Model**

Not every issue needs the same voting system.

Use different models:

1. **Local majority vote**
   For simple local priorities.

2. **Consensus attempt**
   For sensitive social issues.

3. **Affected-community veto**
   If a decision harms a directly affected oppressed community, their voice must carry special weight.

4. **Rights-protected democracy**
   Majority cannot vote away basic rights.

5. **Participatory budgeting vote**
   People rank projects.

6. **Deliberative assembly**
   Randomly selected citizens review complex policies.

7. **Expert + public model**
   Experts explain; people decide.

This prevents simple majoritarianism.
